<p><i><b>A Quiet Place</b></i> is a 2018 American <a href="/wiki/Apocalyptic_and_post-apocalyptic_fiction" title="Apocalyptic and post-apocalyptic fiction">post-apocalyptic</a> <a href="/wiki/Science_fiction_horror_film" class="mw-redirect" title="Science fiction horror film">science fiction horror film</a> co-written, directed by and starring <a href="/wiki/John_Krasinski" title="John Krasinski">John Krasinski</a>. Written by <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Woods" title="Bryan Woods">Bryan Woods</a>, <a href="/wiki/Scott_Beck" title="Scott Beck">Scott Beck</a> and Krasinski, the plot revolves around a father (Krasinski) and a mother (<a href="/wiki/Emily_Blunt" title="Emily Blunt">Emily Blunt</a>) who struggle to survive and raise their children in a <a href="/wiki/Apocalyptic_and_post-apocalyptic_fiction" title="Apocalyptic and post-apocalyptic fiction">post-apocalyptic</a> world inhabited by blind <a href="/wiki/Extraterrestrial_life" title="Extraterrestrial life">extraterrestrial</a> creatures with an acute sense of hearing.
</p><p>Beck and Woods had begun developing a story that would lead into the film at college. In July 2016, Krasinski read their <a href="/wiki/Spec_script" title="Spec script">spec script</a> and was hired to direct and rewrite the script in March the following year. The film drew inspiration from other films, such as <i><a href="/wiki/Alien_(film)" title="Alien (film)">Alien</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/No_Country_for_Old_Men_(film)" title="No Country for Old Men (film)">No Country for Old Men</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/In_the_Bedroom" title="In the Bedroom">In the Bedroom</a></i>. Krasinski and Blunt were cast in the lead roles in May 2017. Filming took place in <a href="/wiki/Upstate_New_York" title="Upstate New York">upstate New York</a> from May to November 2017.
</p><p><i>A Quiet Place</i> premiered at <a href="/wiki/South_by_Southwest" title="South by Southwest">South by Southwest</a> on March 9, 2018, and was released in the United States on April 6, 2018, by <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Pictures" title="Paramount Pictures">Paramount Pictures</a>. It became a major box office success, grossing over $340 million worldwide, and received critical acclaim for its atmosphere, direction, acting, and sound design. It was described as a "smart, wickedly frightening good time",<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> and chosen by both the <a href="/wiki/National_Board_of_Review_Awards_2018#Top_10_Films" title="National Board of Review Awards 2018">National Board of Review</a> and <a href="/wiki/American_Film_Institute_Awards_2018#Top_10_Films" title="American Film Institute Awards 2018">American Film Institute</a> as one of the top ten films of 2018.
</p><p>The film received several award nominations, including a <a href="/wiki/Golden_Globe_Award_for_Best_Original_Score" title="Golden Globe Award for Best Original Score">Golden Globe Award for Best Original Score</a>, <a href="/wiki/Producers_Guild_of_America_Award_for_Best_Theatrical_Motion_Picture" title="Producers Guild of America Award for Best Theatrical Motion Picture">Producers Guild of America Award for Best Theatrical Motion Picture</a>, <a href="/wiki/Writers_Guild_of_America_Award_for_Best_Original_Screenplay" title="Writers Guild of America Award for Best Original Screenplay">Writers Guild of America Award for Best Original Screenplay</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Screen_Actors_Guild_Award_for_Outstanding_Performance_by_a_Female_Actor_in_a_Supporting_Role" title="Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Female Actor in a Supporting Role">Screen Actors Guild Award for Outstanding Performance by a Female Actor in a Supporting Role</a> for Blunt, which she later won. It was also nominated for a <a href="/wiki/BAFTA_Award_for_Best_Sound" title="BAFTA Award for Best Sound">BAFTA Award for Best Sound</a> at the <a href="/wiki/72nd_British_Academy_Film_Awards" title="72nd British Academy Film Awards">72nd BAFTA Awards</a> as well for a <a href="/wiki/Academy_Award_for_Best_Sound_Editing" title="Academy Award for Best Sound Editing">Academy Award for Best Sound Editing</a> at the <a href="/wiki/91st_Academy_Awards" title="91st Academy Awards">91st Academy Awards</a>.
</p><p>A sequel, <i><a href="/wiki/A_Quiet_Place_Part_II" title="A Quiet Place Part II">A Quiet Place Part II</a></i>, is scheduled to be released on September 4, 2020, with Krasinski again directing and the main cast also returning; the film was originally slated for a March release but was postponed to September due to the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Quiet_Place_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Throughout the year 2020, most of Earth's human and animal populations have been annihilated by sightless extraterrestrial creatures. The creatures, which attack anything that makes noise, have hypersensitive hearing and indestructible, armored skin.
</p><p>The Abbott family&#160;&#8211;&#32;wife Evelyn, husband Lee, congenitally deaf daughter Regan, and sons Marcus and Beau&#160;&#8211;&#32;silently scavenge for supplies in a deserted town. Going barefoot while out in the open, the family communicates in <a href="/wiki/American_Sign_Language" title="American Sign Language">American Sign Language</a>. Four-year-old Beau is drawn to a battery-powered space shuttle toy, but Lee takes it away due to the noise it would make. Regan returns the toy to Beau, who also takes the batteries that his father removed from it. Beau activates the toy when the family is walking home while crossing a bridge, giving away his location to a nearby creature that kills him before Lee can save him.
</p>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:House_used_as_the_Abbott_family_home_in_A_Quiet_Place.jpg" class="image"><img alt="A white wooden three-story house with a scaled mansard roof, lit by the sun from the left, viewed at a three-quarters angle amid a landscape with wooded hills in the background under a clear blue sky" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/House_used_as_the_Abbott_family_home_in_A_Quiet_Place.jpg/220px-House_used_as_the_Abbott_family_home_in_A_Quiet_Place.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="144" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/House_used_as_the_Abbott_family_home_in_A_Quiet_Place.jpg/330px-House_used_as_the_Abbott_family_home_in_A_Quiet_Place.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/House_used_as_the_Abbott_family_home_in_A_Quiet_Place.jpg/440px-House_used_as_the_Abbott_family_home_in_A_Quiet_Place.jpg 2x" data-file-width="5148" data-file-height="3360" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:House_used_as_the_Abbott_family_home_in_A_Quiet_Place.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The house used as the Abbotts' in the film</div></div></div>
<p>Over a year later, Regan struggles with guilt over her brother's death, Evelyn enters the final stages of pregnancy, and Lee fruitlessly tries to make radio contact with the outside world. Lee attempts to upgrade Regan's <a href="/wiki/Cochlear_implant" title="Cochlear implant">cochlear implant</a> with scavenged miniature amplifiers to restore her hearing, but the devices are ineffective. Later, Lee takes Marcus to a nearby river with a large waterfall to teach him how to fish, while Regan abandons her mother to visit Beau's memorial. Lee explains to Marcus that they are safe from the creatures in the presence of louder and constant sounds, as the sounds mask over their voices and do not attract creatures. Marcus then reveals that Regan blames herself for Beau's death and needs her father to tell her he still loves her.
</p><p>Alone at the house, Evelyn goes into labor. While making her way to their basement, she accidentally steps on an exposed nail with her right foot. In pain, Evelyn accidentally drops a glass picture frame and alerts a nearby creature. Evelyn flips a switch that changes the exterior house lights to red as a danger signal to the others and struggles to remain silent during her <a href="/wiki/Uterine_contraction" title="Uterine contraction">contractions</a>.
</p><p>Arriving at the farm and seeing the lights, Lee instructs Marcus to set off fireworks as a diversion. Arriving at the house, Lee finds Evelyn hiding in the bathroom with their newborn son and, along with Evelyn, makes his way to their improvised soundproofed basement. Lee leaves to find the other children, promising Evelyn he will protect them. Evelyn then falls asleep but soon wakes to discover that the barn basement is flooded with water from a broken pipe and that a creature has found its way inside the basement hideaway.
</p><p>Regan, hurrying back to the farm, takes refuge atop a <a href="/wiki/Grain_silo" class="mw-redirect" title="Grain silo">grain silo</a> with Marcus, lighting a fire to alert their father of their whereabouts. However, they run out of lighter fluid and the fire dies before they can attract Lee's attention. A hatch door then suddenly gives way, and Marcus falls into the silo. The sound of the door falling distracts the creature that was stalking Evelyn and it targets Marcus and Regan. Regan, who has jumped in after Marcus, <a href="/wiki/Grain_entrapment" title="Grain entrapment">sinks into the corn and nearly suffocates</a>, but Marcus saves her. Regan's cochlear implant reacts to the proximity of the creature by emitting a high-frequency sound that drives it away. The children proceed to escape from the silo and reunite with their father.
</p><p>The creature returns, attacking and wounding Lee, while Marcus and Regan hide in a pickup truck. After seeing his father wounded, Marcus shouts impulsively, attracting the creature to the truck. Lee signs to Regan that he loves her and always has, before yelling to draw the creature away from his children; the creature, hearing Lee's voice, attacks and kills him. Regan and Marcus roll the truck down a hill to escape and reunite with Evelyn and the baby at the farmhouse.
</p><p>The four retreat to the house's basement.<sup id="cite_ref-METRO2_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-METRO2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> When the creature returns, Regan, who realizes that the sound made by the implant distresses the creature,<sup id="cite_ref-METRO2_6-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-METRO2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> switches the device back on and places it on a nearby microphone, amplifying the <a href="/wiki/Audio_feedback" title="Audio feedback">feedback</a>. Painfully disoriented, the creature exposes the flesh beneath its armored head, allowing Evelyn to fatally shoot it with a <a href="/wiki/Shotgun" title="Shotgun">shotgun</a>. The family views a <a href="/wiki/Closed-circuit_television" title="Closed-circuit television">CCTV</a> monitor, showing two creatures attracted by the noise of the shotgun blast approaching the house. With their newly acquired knowledge of the creatures' weakness, the members of the family arm themselves and prepare to fight back.<sup id="cite_ref-METRO2_6-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-METRO2-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Quiet_Place_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Development_and_writing">Development and writing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Quiet_Place_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Development and writing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>A Quiet Place</i> is a production of <a href="/wiki/Platinum_Dunes" title="Platinum Dunes">Platinum Dunes</a> produced on a budget of $17 million.<sup id="cite_ref-dalessandro_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dalessandro-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Krasinski wrote the screenplay with <a href="/wiki/Scott_Beck" title="Scott Beck">Scott Beck</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bryan_Woods" title="Bryan Woods">Bryan Woods</a>. Beck and Woods grew up together in <a href="/wiki/Iowa" title="Iowa">Iowa</a> and had watched numerous silent films in college, and they began working on the story that led to the film. They used their experience growing up close to farmland as the setting, including a grain silo as a place considered dangerous in their upbringing.
</p><p>Beck and Woods began writing <i>A Quiet Place</i> in January 2016,<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and Krasinski read their initial script the following July.<sup id="cite_ref-miller_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-miller-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> The concept appealed to him of parents protecting their children, especially as his second child with actress <a href="/wiki/Emily_Blunt" title="Emily Blunt">Emily Blunt</a> had just been born,<sup id="cite_ref-fowler_7-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-fowler-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> and Blunt encouraged him to direct the film.<sup id="cite_ref-miller_11-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-miller-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> By March 2017, Paramount had bought Beck and Woods's script, and they hired Krasinski to rewrite the script and direct the film, which was his third directorial credit and his first for a major studio.<sup id="cite_ref-kroll_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kroll-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> Blunt did not want to be cast in the film, but she read the script on a plane flight and immediately told her husband, "I need to do it."<sup id="cite_ref-miller_11-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-miller-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> He agreed, and they were both cast in the starring roles.<sup id="cite_ref-kroll_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-kroll-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Filming">Filming</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Quiet_Place_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Filming">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright"><div class="thumbinner" style="width:222px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Wallkill_River_Bridge.jpg" class="image"><img alt="A photo (taken in daytime) of some of a bridge which is near vegetation" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Wallkill_River_Bridge.jpg/220px-Wallkill_River_Bridge.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="165" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Wallkill_River_Bridge.jpg/330px-Wallkill_River_Bridge.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/95/Wallkill_River_Bridge.jpg/440px-Wallkill_River_Bridge.jpg 2x" data-file-width="1600" data-file-height="1200" /></a>  <div class="thumbcaption"><div class="magnify"><a href="/wiki/File:Wallkill_River_Bridge.jpg" class="internal" title="Enlarge"></a></div>The Springtown Truss Bridge on the <a href="/wiki/Wallkill_Valley_Rail_Trail" title="Wallkill Valley Rail Trail">Wallkill Valley Rail Trail</a>, used as a location in the film</div></div></div>
<p>Production took place from May to November 2017 in <a href="/wiki/Dutchess_County,_New_York" title="Dutchess County, New York">Dutchess</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ulster_County,_New_York" title="Ulster County, New York">Ulster</a> counties in <a href="/wiki/Upstate_New_York" title="Upstate New York">upstate New York</a>. Filmmakers spent their budget locally, including a purchase of <span class="nowrap">20 tons</span> of corn, which they hired local farmers to grow. Some filming took place on a soundstage in the town of <a href="/wiki/Pawling_(town),_New_York" title="Pawling (town), New York">Pawling</a> in Dutchess County, as well as on-location in the county's city of <a href="/wiki/Beacon,_New_York" title="Beacon, New York">Beacon</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> Filming also took place on the <a href="/wiki/Wallkill_Valley_Rail_Trail" title="Wallkill Valley Rail Trail">Wallkill Valley Rail Trail</a> in <a href="/wiki/New_Paltz,_New_York" title="New Paltz, New York">New Paltz</a> of Ulster County, using the Springtown Truss Bridge.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> Outside Dutchess and Ulster counties, filming took place on Main Street in <a href="/wiki/Little_Falls,_New_York" class="mw-redirect" title="Little Falls, New York">Little Falls</a> in <a href="/wiki/Herkimer_County,_New_York" title="Herkimer County, New York">Herkimer County, New York</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Sound_and_music">Sound and music</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Quiet_Place_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Sound and music">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>During filming, the crew avoided making noise so <a href="/wiki/Diegetic_music#Film_sound_and_music" title="Diegetic music">diegetic synchronized sounds</a> (e.g., the sound of rolling dice on a game board) could be recorded; the sounds were amplified in post-production. A traditional musical score was also added, which Krasinski justified in wanting audiences to remain familiar with watching a mainstream film, and not feel like part of a "silence experiment."<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Supervising sound editors <a href="/wiki/Erik_Aadahl" title="Erik Aadahl">Erik Aadahl</a> and <a href="/wiki/Ethan_Van_der_Ryn" title="Ethan Van der Ryn">Ethan Van der Ryn</a> worked on <i>A Quiet Place</i>. For scenes from the perspective of the deaf daughter, sound was removed to put greater focus on the visual. They also advised on organizing shots to reflect the creatures' perspective, like showing them noticing a sound, then showing what was causing the sound. Composer <a href="/wiki/Marco_Beltrami" title="Marco Beltrami">Marco Beltrami</a> provided the sound editors music to work with in a way that would not interfere with the sound design throughout the film.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the film, creatures are blind and communicate through clicking sounds. Aadahl and Van der Ryn said they were inspired by <a href="/wiki/Animal_echolocation" title="Animal echolocation">animal echolocation</a>, such as that employed by bats. The sound of feedback, normally avoided by sound editors, was woven into the story at a loudness level that would not bother audiences too much.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Use_of_sign_language">Use of sign language</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Quiet_Place_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Use of sign language">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The characters communicate in <a href="/wiki/American_Sign_Language" title="American Sign Language">American Sign Language</a> (ASL) to avoid making sound, so the filmmakers hired deaf mentor Douglas Ridloff to teach ASL to the actors and to be available to make corrections. They also hired an ASL interpreter for deaf actress Simmonds, so that spoken and signed language could be interpreted back and forth on set.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> Simmonds grew up with ASL, and she helped teach her fellow actors to sign.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> She said, "In the movie, we've been signing together for years and years. So it should look fluent."<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> She observed that each character's use of sign language reflected his or her motivations: the father had short and brief signs which showed his survival mentality, while the mother had more expressive signs as part of wanting her children to experience more than survival.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> Krasinski said that Simmonds's character used "signing that's very defiant, it's very teenage defiant."<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Simmonds said that she suggested for the daughter to rebel rather than cower during a sign-language fight with her father. She also said that the script originally had the father sign "I love you" to his daughter at the end of the film, but she suggested for him to follow with "I've always loved you" to make up for their arguing earlier in the film.<sup id="cite_ref-METRO_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-METRO-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Producers <a href="/wiki/Andrew_Form" title="Andrew Form">Andrew Form</a> and <a href="/wiki/Bradley_Fuller" class="mw-redirect" title="Bradley Fuller">Bradley Fuller</a> said that they initially planned not to provide on-screen subtitles for sign-language dialogue while providing only "context clues," but they realized that subtitles were necessary for the scene in which the deaf daughter and her hearing father argue about the modified hearing aid. They subsequently added subtitles for all sign-language dialogue in the film.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Creature_design">Creature design</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Quiet_Place_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Creature design">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Production designer <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Beecroft" title="Jeffrey Beecroft">Jeffrey Beecroft</a> headed the creature design, and <a href="/wiki/Industrial_Light_%26_Magic" title="Industrial Light &amp; Magic">Industrial Light &amp; Magic</a> created the creatures, led by visual effects supervisor <a href="/wiki/Scott_Farrar" title="Scott Farrar">Scott Farrar</a>. The director wanted the creatures to look like they had evolved to no longer need eyes, and to be "somewhat humanoid" in nature. Farrar said the initial creature design showed them with rhinoceros-like horns out of their faces, later redesigned. <i><a href="/wiki/Vanity_Fair_(magazine)" title="Vanity Fair (magazine)">Vanity Fair</a></i> reported, "The team immediately set about pulling references; prehistoric fish, black snakes, and bats, particularly their movement patterns. Inspiration was also drawn from bog people: cadavers that have been mummified in peat, turning the skin black and giving it a sagging, leathery look."<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> Krasinski provided motion-capture for the creatures.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Quiet_Place_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/Paramount_Pictures" title="Paramount Pictures">Paramount Pictures</a> released the first trailer for <i>A Quiet Place</i> in November 2017.<sup id="cite_ref-mcnary_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mcnary-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> It aired a 30-second commercial for the film during the US football championship <a href="/wiki/Super_Bowl_LII" title="Super Bowl LII">Super Bowl LII</a> on February 4, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Of the seven trailers that aired during the playoff, <i>A Quiet Place</i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Red_Sparrow" title="Red Sparrow">Red Sparrow</a></i> were shown during the pregame and had the lowest views and social conversations. <i>A Quiet Place</i> had 149,000 views on <a href="/wiki/YouTube" title="YouTube">YouTube</a>, 275,000 views on <a href="/wiki/Facebook" title="Facebook">Facebook</a>, and 2,900 social conversations.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup> On February 12, 2018, Krasinski appeared on <i><a href="/wiki/The_Ellen_DeGeneres_Show" title="The Ellen DeGeneres Show">The Ellen DeGeneres Show</a></i> to present the full trailer for <i>A Quiet Place</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> The studio spent an estimated $86 million on prints and advertisements for the film.<sup id="cite_ref-profit_33-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-profit-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Quiet_Place_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Premiere">Premiere</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Quiet_Place_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Premiere">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>A Quiet Place</i> premiered at the <a href="/wiki/South_by_Southwest" title="South by Southwest">South by Southwest</a> Film Festival as the opening-night film on <span class="nowrap">March 9</span>, 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> It was selected from 2,458 submissions,<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> and earned "raves" from critics, according to <i><a href="/wiki/IndieWire" title="IndieWire">IndieWire</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> Following its premiere, the film experienced social media growth to under <span class="nowrap">52 million</span> views across multiple platforms, outpacing <i><a href="/wiki/Get_Out" title="Get Out">Get Out</a></i> (2017), which had <span class="nowrap">46.9 million</span> views.<sup id="cite_ref-dalessandro_9-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dalessandro-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office_forecast">Box office forecast</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Quiet_Place_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Box office forecast">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>The Tracking Board</i> reported on <span class="nowrap">March 14</span>, "The stellar reviews out of SXSW, coupled with the fact that there isn't anything like it in the marketplace, should help it stand out among its bigger-budget competition."<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Deadline_Hollywood" title="Deadline Hollywood">Deadline Hollywood</a></i> said on <span class="nowrap">March 15</span> that the film was projected to gross around $20 million in its opening weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-dalessandro_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-dalessandro-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i> reported on <span class="nowrap">March 27</span> that the film "is tracking to open between" <span class="nowrap">$16 million</span> and <span class="nowrap">$30 million</span>,<sup id="cite_ref-lang_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lang-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup> which reached a basement of low-$20 million by the week of its release.<sup id="cite_ref-39" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/BoxOffice_(magazine)" class="mw-redirect" title="BoxOffice (magazine)">BoxOffice</a></i> initially estimated on <span class="nowrap">February 9</span>, 2018 that <i>A Quiet Place</i> would gross <span class="nowrap">$17 million</span> in its opening weekend, and that it would gross a total of <span class="nowrap">$60 million</span> in the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-boxoffice20180209_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boxoffice20180209-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> By <span class="nowrap">March 30</span>, it increased its estimate to an opening weekend gross of <span class="nowrap">$27.5 million</span> and a US total gross of <span class="nowrap">$85 million</span>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> The magazine said the film's trailer was well-received online and that it appeared frequently in previews for <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Wars:_The_Last_Jedi" title="Star Wars: The Last Jedi">Star Wars: The Last Jedi</a></i>. <i>BoxOffice</i> wrote, "The horror genre has also shown a knack for over-performing against expectations at the box office in recent years, setting this release up for potential success." It added that <i>A Quiet Place</i> would have to compete against another horror film, <i><a href="/wiki/Truth_or_Dare_(2018_film)" title="Truth or Dare (2018 film)">Truth or Dare</a></i>, which would be released the following weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-boxoffice20180209_40-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-boxoffice20180209-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> The magazine's staff drew "very favorable" comparisons between <i>A Quiet Place</i> and the 2016 films <i><a href="/wiki/10_Cloverfield_Lane" title="10 Cloverfield Lane">10 Cloverfield Lane</a></i> and <i><a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Breathe" title="Don&#39;t Breathe">Don't Breathe</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Theatrical_run">Theatrical run</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Quiet_Place_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Theatrical run">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>A Quiet Place</i> was first commercially released in theaters in the first week of April 2018 in multiple territories, including North America.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup> The film grossed $188 million in the United States and Canada, and $152.7 million in other territories, for a worldwide total of $340.7 million.<sup id="cite_ref-BOM_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BOM-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/Deadline_Hollywood" title="Deadline Hollywood">Deadline Hollywood</a></i> estimates the net profit of the film to be $93 million, when factoring together all expenses and revenues.<sup id="cite_ref-profit_33-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-profit-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Paramount Pictures released the film in <span class="nowrap">3,508 theaters</span> in the United States and Canada on <span class="nowrap">April 6</span>, 2018, alongside <i><a href="/wiki/Blockers_(film)" title="Blockers (film)">Blockers</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Chappaquiddick_(film)" title="Chappaquiddick (film)">Chappaquiddick</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Miracle_Season" title="The Miracle Season">The Miracle Season</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-rubin_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rubin-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> The film made $19 million on its first day (including $4.3 million from Thursday night previews at <span class="nowrap">2,750 theaters</span>), increasing weekend projections to $47 million. Unlike most horror films, which are front-loaded on Friday and suffer drops the rest of the weekend, <i>A Quiet Place</i> made $19.1 million on Saturday. It went on to debut to $50.2 million, topping the box office and marking the biggest opening for a Paramount film since <i><a href="/wiki/Star_Trek_Beyond" title="Star Trek Beyond">Star Trek Beyond</a></i> in July 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-opening_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-opening-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The film made $32.9 million in its second weekend, dropping 34% (better than the 50+% that horror films normally see) and finishing second at the box office, behind newcomer <i><a href="/wiki/Rampage_(2018_film)" title="Rampage (2018 film)">Rampage</a></i> ($35.7 million).<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> The hold represented the second-best-ever second weekend for a scary movie behind <i><a href="/wiki/It_(2017_film)" title="It (2017 film)">It</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> The film regained the top spot the following week, grossing $20.9 million (a 36% drop),<sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup> but dropped back down to second place the following weekend behind newcomer <i><a href="/wiki/Avengers:_Infinity_War" title="Avengers: Infinity War">Avengers: Infinity War</a></i> with $10.7 million.<sup id="cite_ref-49" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Through its first two weeks of international release, the film had made $51.7 million, with its top markets being the United Kingdom ($9.2 million), Mexico ($5.1 million), Australia ($4.6 million), Brazil ($3.9 million), Indonesia ($3.4 million), the Philippines ($2.7 million)<sup id="cite_ref-50" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> and Taiwan ($1.9 million). It also debuted to $2.2 million in Russia, the biggest-ever opening for a Paramount horror film in the country.<sup id="cite_ref-51" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup> In its third weekend overseas, it dipped only 37% for a total of $15 million from 57 markets.<sup id="cite_ref-Tartaglione_52-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tartaglione-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> In its fourth weekend in international markets, it grossed $6.6 million.<sup id="cite_ref-Tartaglione_52-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tartaglione-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup> As of May 20, 2018, the film's largest markets were United Kingdom ($16.3 million), Australia ($9.3 million), Mexico ($7.5 million) and Brazil ($6.9 million).<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup> The film was released in China on May 18 and made $17.7 million from 8,731 screens in its opening weekend.<sup id="cite_ref-55" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-55">&#91;55&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Quiet_Place_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_response">Critical response</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Quiet_Place_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=15" title="Edit section: Critical response">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">review aggregator</a> website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, the film holds an approval rating of 95% based on 367 reviews, and an <a href="/wiki/Weighted_arithmetic_mean" title="Weighted arithmetic mean">average rating</a> of 8.17/10. The website's critical consensus reads, "<i>A Quiet Place</i> artfully plays on elemental fears with a ruthlessly intelligent creature feature that's as original as it is scary – and establishes director John Krasinski as a rising talent."<sup id="cite_ref-56" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-56">&#91;56&#93;</a></sup> On <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, the film has a weighted average score of 82 out of 100, based on 55 critics, indicating "universal acclaim".<sup id="cite_ref-57" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-57">&#91;57&#93;</a></sup> Audiences polled by <a href="/wiki/CinemaScore" title="CinemaScore">CinemaScore</a> gave the film an average grade of "B+" on an A+ to F scale, while <a href="/wiki/PostTrak" title="PostTrak">PostTrak</a> reported filmgoers gave it an 81% overall positive score and a 63% "definite recommend".<sup id="cite_ref-58" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-58">&#91;58&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Writing for <i><a href="/wiki/Rolling_Stone" title="Rolling Stone">Rolling Stone</a></i>, <a href="/wiki/Peter_Travers" title="Peter Travers">Peter Travers</a> gave the film 3.5 out of 4, saying, "The question Krasinski tackles is what defines a family and what's needed to preserve it? 'Who are we', asks Mom, 'if we can't protect our children?' The answers are worked out with satisfying complexity and genuine feeling, proving indeed that home is where family is. This new horror classic will fry your nerves to a frazzle."<sup id="cite_ref-59" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-59">&#91;59&#93;</a></sup> <i><a href="/wiki/The_Hollywood_Reporter" title="The Hollywood Reporter">The Hollywood Reporter</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s John DeFore described the film as "a terrifying thriller with a surprisingly warm heart" and said, "You might have to go back to Jeff Nichols' 2011 <i><a href="/wiki/Take_Shelter" title="Take Shelter">Take Shelter</a></i> to find a film that has used the fantastic this well to convey the combination of fear and responsibility a good parent feels."<sup id="cite_ref-60" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-60">&#91;60&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><i><a href="/wiki/Variety_(magazine)" title="Variety (magazine)">Variety</a></i><span class="nowrap" style="padding-left:0.1em;">&#39;</span>s <a href="/wiki/Owen_Gleiberman" title="Owen Gleiberman">Owen Gleiberman</a> said, "<i>A Quiet Place</i> is a tautly original genre-bending exercise, technically sleek and accomplished, with some vivid, scary moments, though it's a little too in love with the stoned logic of its own premise."<sup id="cite_ref-gleiberman_61-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-gleiberman-61">&#91;61&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Michael_Phillips_(critic)" title="Michael Phillips (critic)">Michael Phillips</a> of the <i><a href="/wiki/Chicago_Tribune" title="Chicago Tribune">Chicago Tribune</a></i> gave the film 2.5 out of 4 and said, "My favorite moment, an encounter between Regan and one of the monsters in a cornfield, plays with sound and image and tension, creatively. Other bits are more shameless...I don't know if I'd call <i>A Quiet Place</i> enjoyable; it's more grueling than cathartic."<sup id="cite_ref-62" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-62">&#91;62&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Author <a href="/wiki/Stephen_King" title="Stephen King">Stephen King</a> praised the film in a <a href="/wiki/Twitter" title="Twitter">tweet</a>, saying, "A QUIET PLACE is an extraordinary piece of work. Terrific acting, but the main thing is the SILENCE, and how it makes the camera's eye open wide in a way few movies manage."<sup id="cite_ref-63" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-63">&#91;63&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Nick Allen of <a href="/wiki/Roger_Ebert" title="Roger Ebert">RogerEbert.com</a> called <i>A Quiet Place</i> "Krasinski's breakthrough as a triple-threat entertainer, but it's been a long time coming... By no accident, he's tackled the horror genre while relying on the unique strength that can be seen throughout his acting work, and one that has made him relatable as an everyman across TV and film—expressive silence."<sup id="cite_ref-64" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-64">&#91;64&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Peter_Bradshaw" title="Peter Bradshaw">Peter Bradshaw</a> of <i><a href="/wiki/The_Guardian" title="The Guardian">The Guardian</a></i> says: "In its simplicity and punch, this is a film that feels as if it could have been made decades ago, in the classic age of <i><a href="/wiki/Planet_of_the_Apes" title="Planet of the Apes">Planet of the Apes</a></i> or <i><a href="/wiki/The_Omega_Man" title="The Omega Man">The Omega Man</a></i>. It is a cracking back-to-basics thriller that does not depend too much on what these creatures look like."<sup id="cite_ref-65" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-65">&#91;65&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Commentary_and_themes">Commentary and themes</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Quiet_Place_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=17" title="Edit section: Commentary and themes">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Speaking of the political and social commentary the film encouraged, Krasinski said, "The best compliment you can get on any movie is that it starts a conversation. The fact that people are leaving and talking about anything is really fun—but certainly about deep stuff like that, is awesome."<sup id="cite_ref-83" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-83">&#91;83&#93;</a></sup> Krasinski, who did not grow up with horror films, said that prior films of the genre such as <i><a href="/wiki/Don%27t_Breathe" title="Don&#39;t Breathe">Don't Breathe</a></i> (2016) and <i><a href="/wiki/Get_Out" title="Get Out">Get Out</a></i> (2017) that had societal commentary were part of his research. In addition to considering his film a metaphor for parenthood, he compared the premise to US politics in 2018, "I think in our political situation, that's what's going on now: You can close your eyes and stick your head in the sand, or you can try to participate in whatever's going on." He cited <i><a href="/wiki/Jaws_(film)" title="Jaws (film)">Jaws</a></i> (1975) as an influence, with how the protagonist police officer moved from New York to an island to avoid frightening situations, and was forced to encounter one in his new location with shark attacks.<sup id="cite_ref-84" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-84">&#91;84&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Matthew Monagle of <i><a href="/wiki/Film_School_Rejects" title="Film School Rejects">Film School Rejects</a></i> said <i>A Quiet Place</i> seemed to be "the early frontrunner for the sparsely intellectual horror movie of the year", like previous films <i><a href="/wiki/The_Babadook" title="The Babadook">The Babadook</a></i> (2014) and <i><a href="/wiki/The_Witch_(2015_film)" title="The Witch (2015 film)">The Witch</a></i> (2015). Monagle said Krasinski, who had directed two previous films, was "making an unusual pivot into a genre typically reserved for newcomers", and considered it to be part of a movement toward horror films layered "in storytelling, [with] character beats not typically found in a horror movie".<sup id="cite_ref-85" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-85">&#91;85&#93;</a></sup> Tatiana Tenreyro, writing for <i><a href="/wiki/Bustle_(magazine)" title="Bustle (magazine)">Bustle</a></i>, said while <i>A Quiet Place</i> was not a <a href="/wiki/Silent_film" title="Silent film">silent film</a>, "It is the first of its kind within the modern horror genre for how little spoken dialogue it actually has." She said the rare moments of spoken dialogue "give depth to this horror movie, showing how the narrative defies the genre's traditional films even further".<sup id="cite_ref-86" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-86">&#91;86&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Roman Catholic Bishop <a href="/wiki/Robert_Barron_(bishop)" class="mw-redirect" title="Robert Barron (bishop)">Robert Barron</a> was surprised by what he saw as strikingly religious themes in the film. He likened the family's primitive, agrarian life of silence to monasticism, and commends their self-giving love. Barron suggested <a href="/wiki/Pro-life" class="mw-redirect" title="Pro-life">pro-life</a> themes, especially in the choices of the parents, as Mrs. Abbott risks everything to give birth to a child, and her husband lays down his own life so that the children can live: what Barron sees as the ultimate expression of parental love.<sup id="cite_ref-87" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-87">&#91;87&#93;</a></sup> Sonny Bunch of the <i><a href="/wiki/Washington_Post" class="mw-redirect" title="Washington Post">Washington Post</a></i> also commented and expanded on a pro-life message.<sup id="cite_ref-88" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-88">&#91;88&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Krasinski, who had recently become a father, said in a conference interview "I was already in a state of terror about whether or not I was a good enough father," and added that the meaning of parenthood had been elevated for him by imagining being a father in a nightmare world, struggling to simply keep his children alive.<sup id="cite_ref-89" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-89">&#91;89&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Krasinski has told CBS News "The scares were secondary to how powerful this could be as an allegory or metaphor for parenthood. For me, this is all about parenthood."
</p><p><a href="/wiki/Richard_Brody" title="Richard Brody">Richard Brody</a>, writing for <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Yorker" title="The New Yorker">The New Yorker</a></i>, criticized <i>A Quiet Place</i> for what he perceived to be the inclusion of <a href="/wiki/Conservatism" title="Conservatism">conservative</a> and pro-gun themes in the film. He described it as "the antithesis of ‘Get Out’" and "both apparently unconscious and conspicuously regressive" and opined that it "[brings] to the fore the idealistic elements of gun culture while dramatizing the tragic implications that inevitably shadow that idealism."<sup id="cite_ref-90" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-90">&#91;90&#93;</a></sup> Krasinski addressed Brody's criticism in a subsequent interview with <i><a href="/wiki/Esquire_(magazine)" title="Esquire (magazine)">Esquire</a></i>, stating that he did not write the film with an intentional political message. Krasinski said, "I never saw it that way or ever thought of it until it was presented to me in that way. [The film] wasn't about being, you know, silent and political... my whole metaphor was solely about parenthood." <sup id="cite_ref-91" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-91">&#91;91&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sequel">Sequel</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=A_Quiet_Place_(film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=19" title="Edit section: Sequel">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/A_Quiet_Place_Part_II" title="A Quiet Place Part II">A Quiet Place Part II</a></div>
<p><i>A Quiet Place Part II</i> is an upcoming American <a href="/wiki/Horror_film" title="Horror film">horror film</a> that is the sequel to <i>A Quiet Place</i>. The sequel film, written and directed by Krasinski, stars Blunt, Simmonds, Jupe, <a href="/wiki/Cillian_Murphy" title="Cillian Murphy">Cillian Murphy</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Djimon_Hounsou" title="Djimon Hounsou">Djimon Hounsou</a>. Production took place in <a href="/wiki/Western_New_York" title="Western New York">Western New York</a> from June 2019 to September 2019. <a href="/wiki/Paramount_Pictures" title="Paramount Pictures">Paramount Pictures</a> planned to release <i>A Quiet Place Part II</i> in theaters on March 20, 2020, however the film was rescheduled to September 4, 2020 due to the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">COVID-19 pandemic</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-93" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-93">&#91;93&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
