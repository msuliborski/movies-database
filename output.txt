<p><i><b>Rush</b></i> is a 2013 <a href="/wiki/Biographical_film" title="Biographical film">biographical</a> <a href="/wiki/Sports_film" title="Sports film">sports film</a> centred on the <a href="/wiki/Hunt%E2%80%93Lauda_rivalry" title="Hunt–Lauda rivalry">Hunt–Lauda rivalry</a> between two <a href="/wiki/Formula_One_drivers" class="mw-redirect" title="Formula One drivers">Formula One drivers</a>, the British <a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a> and the Austrian <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a><sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> during the <a href="/wiki/1976_Formula_One_season" title="1976 Formula One season">1976 Formula 1</a> motor-racing season. It was written by <a href="/wiki/Peter_Morgan" title="Peter Morgan">Peter Morgan</a>, directed by <a href="/wiki/Ron_Howard" title="Ron Howard">Ron Howard</a> and stars <a href="/wiki/Chris_Hemsworth" title="Chris Hemsworth">Chris Hemsworth</a> as Hunt and <a href="/wiki/Daniel_Br%C3%BChl" title="Daniel Brühl">Daniel Brühl</a> as Lauda. The film premiered in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a> on 2 September 2013 and was shown at the <a href="/wiki/2013_Toronto_International_Film_Festival" title="2013 Toronto International Film Festival">2013 Toronto International Film Festival</a><sup id="cite_ref-TIFF2013_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TIFF2013-11">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Evans2013_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Evans2013-12">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> before its United Kingdom release on 13 September 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-release_date_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-release_date-13">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rush_(2013_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/James_Hunt" title="James Hunt">James Hunt</a> and <a href="/wiki/Niki_Lauda" title="Niki Lauda">Niki Lauda</a> are exceptional racing car drivers who first develop a fierce rivalry in 1970 at a <a href="/wiki/British_Formula_3_International_Series" title="British Formula 3 International Series">Formula Three</a> race in <a href="/wiki/London" title="London">London</a>, when both their cars spin before Hunt wins the race. Hunt is a brash and self-confident individual, while Lauda is a cool and calculating technical genius who relies on practice and precision. Lauda takes a large bank loan from Austria’s <a href="/wiki/Raiffeisen_Bank_International" title="Raiffeisen Bank International">Raiffeisen Bank</a> (wearing their logo thereafter) to buy his way into the <a href="/wiki/British_Racing_Motors" title="British Racing Motors">BRM</a> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> team, meeting teammate <a href="/wiki/Clay_Regazzoni" title="Clay Regazzoni">Clay Regazzoni</a> for the first time. Meanwhile, <a href="/wiki/Hesketh_Racing" title="Hesketh Racing">Hesketh Racing</a>, the fledgling racing team Hunt drives for, enters Formula One. Lauda then joins <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Scuderia Ferrari</a> with Regazzoni and wins his first championship in <a href="/wiki/1975_Formula_One_season" title="1975 Formula One season">1975</a>. Hesketh closes down after failing to secure a sponsor, but Hunt joins the <a href="/wiki/McLaren" title="McLaren">McLaren</a> team. During this time, Hunt marries supermodel <a href="/wiki/Suzy_Miller" title="Suzy Miller">Suzy Miller</a>, while Lauda develops a relationship with German socialite Marlene Knaus.
</p><p>The <a href="/wiki/1976_Formula_One_season" title="1976 Formula One season">1976 season</a> starts with Lauda dominating the first two races while Hunt struggles to catch up. Hunt wins the <a href="/wiki/1976_Spanish_Grand_Prix" title="1976 Spanish Grand Prix">Spanish Grand Prix</a>, but is disqualified after a post-race inspection rules that his car is fractionally too wide. Struggling to comply with F1 rules, McLaren suffers a series of racing setbacks, and Hunt's situation is further exacerbated when Suzy starts a relationship with actor <a href="/wiki/Richard_Burton" title="Richard Burton">Richard Burton</a>. Following his divorce, Hunt regains his competitive spirit and, when his disqualification in Spain is overturned, the points put him into championship contention. Lauda marries Marlene in a private ceremony but begins to have concerns about the effects of his newfound happiness, worrying that he has become vulnerable as a racer, as he now has something to lose.
</p><p>At the <a href="/wiki/1976_German_Grand_Prix" title="1976 German Grand Prix">German Grand Prix</a>, Lauda urges the F1 committee to cancel the race due to heavy rain on the already notoriously dangerous <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring Nordschleife</a>. At a drivers' meeting on race day, Hunt argues that Lauda is trying to benefit by having one less race in the season, and the drivers vote to race. Most drivers start the race with wet weather tyres, which becomes a costly tactic due to most of the track quickly drying. They all change tyres during the second lap, but on the third lap, a suspension arm in Lauda's Ferrari breaks, sending the car flying into an embankment then bursting into flames. Lauda is airlifted to hospital with third-degree burns to his head and face and internal burns to his lungs. For six weeks, Lauda is treated for his injuries while he watches Hunt dominate the races in his absence. Despite his doctor's orders, he decides to return to drive his Ferrari at the <a href="/wiki/1976_Italian_Grand_Prix" title="1976 Italian Grand Prix">Italian Grand Prix</a>, finishing fourth while Hunt fails to finish.
</p><p>The 1976 season comes to a climax at the rain-soaked <a href="/wiki/1976_Japanese_Grand_Prix" title="1976 Japanese Grand Prix">Japanese Grand Prix</a>. Hunt's late rally in Lauda's absence has pulled him within three points of Lauda. At the end of the second lap, after his car has slid several times, Lauda returns to the pits and decides to retire from the race, considering it far too dangerous and opting to stay with Marlene instead. This gives Hunt a chance to win the championship if he can finish third or better. After facing stiff competition under gruelling conditions, tyre problems and a hand injury due to the gear shifter knob breaking, Hunt finishes third, winning the championship by a single point.
</p><p>Hunt spends the rest of the year revelling with fame, sex and drugs, while Lauda takes an interest in flying private planes. At a private airfield in <a href="/wiki/Bologna" title="Bologna">Bologna</a>, Lauda suggests to Hunt that he focus on the next racing season to defend his title, but Hunt argues that his glamorous lifestyle is the most enjoyable part of being world champion. Lauda later on realises that Hunt no longer feels he needs to prove himself to anyone. Hunt continues to race until his retirement in 1979, and becomes a motorsport broadcast commentator until his death in 1993 at the age of 45.
</p><p>Lauda reflects on how their great rivalry and personality differences spurred each other on to their finest achievements, and states that Hunt was the only other driver he ever envied.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Production">Production</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rush_(2013_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Production">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>The film was shot on location in the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>, <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> and <a href="/wiki/Austria" title="Austria">Austria</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-official_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-official-14">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Blackbushe_Airport" title="Blackbushe Airport">Blackbushe Airport</a> in <a href="/wiki/Hampshire" title="Hampshire">Hampshire</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Snetterton_Motor_Racing_Circuit" class="mw-redirect" title="Snetterton Motor Racing Circuit">Snetterton</a> (<a href="/wiki/Norfolk" title="Norfolk">Norfolk</a>), <a href="/wiki/Cadwell_Park" title="Cadwell Park">Cadwell Park</a> (<a href="/wiki/Lincolnshire" title="Lincolnshire">Lincolnshire</a>), the former <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_circuit" title="Crystal Palace circuit">Crystal Palace</a><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Brands_Hatch" title="Brands Hatch">Brands Hatch</a> (<a href="/wiki/Kent" title="Kent">Kent</a>)<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> motor racing circuits in Britain, and at the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a> in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> Both vintage racing cars and replicas were used in the filming.
</p><p>The financiers include <a href="/wiki/H%C3%BCrth" title="Hürth">Hürth</a>-based action concept Film- und Stuntproduktion, Egoli Tossell Film, Revolution Films (GB) and <a href="/wiki/Cross_Creek_Pictures" title="Cross Creek Pictures">Cross Creek Pictures</a> (US). The Film- und Medienstiftung <a href="/wiki/North_Rhine-Westphalia" title="North Rhine-Westphalia">NRW</a> funded the film with €1.35 million, additional funding was provided by MFG Filmförderung <a href="/wiki/Baden-W%C3%BCrttemberg" title="Baden-Württemberg">Baden-Württemberg</a> and the German Federal Film Fund (DFFF).<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Director Ron Howard originally intended for <a href="/wiki/Russell_Crowe" title="Russell Crowe">Russell Crowe</a> to make a cameo appearance as <a href="/wiki/Richard_Burton" title="Richard Burton">Richard Burton</a> for a brief scene where he confronts James Hunt on his affair with Suzy.<sup id="cite_ref-HistoryvsHollywood_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HistoryvsHollywood-19">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Historical_accuracy">Historical accuracy</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rush_(2013_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Historical accuracy">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>Some things in the film are exaggerated (like the <a href="/wiki/Hunt%E2%80%93Lauda_rivalry" title="Hunt–Lauda rivalry">Hunt–Lauda rivalry</a>; in reality they had shared a flat early in their careers and were good friends), others downplayed (like Lauda's wife's shock at his disfigurement), and others invented (like Hunt beating up a reporter or the <a href="/wiki/N%C3%BCrburgring" title="Nürburgring">Nürburgring</a> nickname being "the graveyard"; in fact <a href="/wiki/Jackie_Stewart" title="Jackie Stewart">Jackie Stewart</a> had nicknamed it "the Green Hell").<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup> A further inaccuracy is that when Lauda's car was in flames, another driver released his seatbelts and [Bret Lunger]], succeeded in pulling Lauda out of his car, not four as shown in the movie. Other inaccuracies include the British F3 battle at <a href="/wiki/Crystal_Palace_circuit" title="Crystal Palace circuit">Crystal Palace</a>, which in reality was between Hunt and <a href="/wiki/Dave_Morgan_(racing_driver)" title="Dave Morgan (racing driver)">Dave Morgan</a>, and Hunt's overtake on Regazzoni for 3rd place in the Japanese Grand Prix when in the actual race he passed <a href="/wiki/Alan_Jones_(racing_driver)" title="Alan Jones (racing driver)">Alan Jones</a>. Another error in the Japanese Grand Prix is that Regazzoni and <a href="/wiki/Jacques_Laffite" title="Jacques Laffite">Laffite</a> finished fourth and fifth, while in the actual race it was Jones and Regazzoni who finished fourth and fifth. In the end scene an incident is described where Hunt, while being a tv broadcaster, comes to a meet-up with Lauda on a bicycle with a flat tire. In reality this incident happened while Hunt ran out of money and fell into alcohol-addiction. On this day Lauda gave him money to rebuild his life. Hunt, after Lauda gave him money a second time, later fixed his life and got his job as a television broadcaster.<sup id="cite_ref-Bensinger_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bensinger-24">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> The visual blog <i><a href="/wiki/David_McCandless" title="David McCandless">Information is Beautiful</a></i> deduced that, while taking creative licence into account, the film was 82.9% accurate when compared to real-life events, summarizing that there were "a little staging to get Lauda and Hunt in the same locations sometimes, but otherwise [it was] true".<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Soundtrack">Soundtrack</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rush_(2013_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Soundtrack">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Rush_(2013_soundtrack)" title="Rush (2013 soundtrack)">Rush (2013 soundtrack)</a></div>
<p>The film's orchestral score was composed by <a href="/wiki/Hans_Zimmer" title="Hans Zimmer">Hans Zimmer</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The soundtrack includes 1970s rock music by <a href="/wiki/Dave_Edmunds" title="Dave Edmunds">Dave Edmunds</a>, <a href="/wiki/Steve_Winwood" title="Steve Winwood">Steve Winwood</a> (originally performed and written by the <a href="/wiki/Spencer_Davis_Group" class="mw-redirect" title="Spencer Davis Group">Spencer Davis Group</a>), <a href="/wiki/Mud_(band)" title="Mud (band)">Mud</a>, <a href="/wiki/Thin_Lizzy" title="Thin Lizzy">Thin Lizzy</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Bowie" title="David Bowie">David Bowie</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Marketing">Marketing</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rush_(2013_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Marketing">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p><a href="/wiki/BBC_Two" title="BBC Two">BBC Two</a> aired the documentary <i>Hunt vs. Lauda: F1's Greatest Racing Rivals</i>, on 14 July 2013. The documentary provides an extensive look at the rivalry between Hunt and Lauda, featuring interviews with Lauda and former crew members of the McLaren and Ferrari teams.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>The Ferrari &amp; the Cinema Society jointly organised a screening of the film at Chelsea Clearview Cinemas in New York on 18 September 2013. Chris Hemsworth attended the screening.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Reception">Reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rush_(2013_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rush_(2013_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Rush</i> was a box office success. It earned $26.9 million in domestic box office and $71.3 million in international box office for a worldwide gross of $98.2 million against a budget of $38 million.<sup id="cite_ref-box_office_9-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-box_office-9">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rush_(2013_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">review aggregator</a> website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a>, the film holds an approval rating of 88% based on 227 reviews with an average rating of 7.51/10. The website’s critical consensus reads, "A sleek, slick, well-oiled machine, <i>Rush</i> is a finely crafted sports drama with exhilarating race sequences and strong performances from Chris Hemsworth and Daniel Brühl."<sup id="cite_ref-rt_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-rt-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup> Another review aggregator, <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a>, which assigns a <a href="/wiki/Normalization_(statistics)" title="Normalization (statistics)">normalised</a> rating to reviews, calculated an average score of 74 out of 100, based on 43 critics, indicating "generally favorable reviews".<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When Niki Lauda first saw the pre-screening of the unedited footage, he considered himself to be portrayed too negatively. This changed on the day of the first screening when <a href="/wiki/Bernie_Ecclestone" title="Bernie Ecclestone">Bernie Ecclestone</a> told him how much he liked it.<sup id="cite_ref-Bensinger_24-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bensinger-24">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> Lauda was pleased with the overall look of the film. He was quoted as saying: "When I saw it the first time I was impressed. There was no Hollywood changes or things changed a little bit Hollywood-like. It is very accurate. And this really surprised me very positively".<sup id="cite_ref-HistoryvsHollywood_19-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HistoryvsHollywood-19">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Home_media">Home media</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Rush_(2013_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Home media">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><i>Rush</i> was released on <a href="/wiki/DVD" title="DVD">DVD</a> and <a href="/wiki/Blu-ray" title="Blu-ray">Blu-ray</a> on 28 January 2014. A <a href="/wiki/Sainsbury%27s" title="Sainsbury&#39;s">Sainsbury's</a> exclusive edition with a bonus disc of new special features was released for a limited time. The Australian Blu-ray release is bundled with the 2013 documentary <i><a href="/wiki/1_(2013_film)" title="1 (2013 film)">1</a></i>.<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
