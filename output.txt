<p><i><b>Dr. Dolittle</b></i> (also written as <i><b>Doctor Dolittle</b></i>) is a 1998 American <a href="/wiki/Fantasy_film" title="Fantasy film">fantasy</a> <a href="/wiki/Comedy_film" title="Comedy film">comedy film</a> directed by <a href="/wiki/Betty_Thomas" title="Betty Thomas">Betty Thomas</a>, written by Larry Levin and <a href="/wiki/Nat_Mauldin" title="Nat Mauldin">Nat Mauldin</a>, and starring <a href="/wiki/Eddie_Murphy" title="Eddie Murphy">Eddie Murphy</a> in the titular role. The film was based on the series of <a href="/wiki/Dr._Dolittle" class="mw-redirect" title="Dr. Dolittle">children's stories of the same name</a> by <a href="/wiki/Hugh_Lofting" title="Hugh Lofting">Hugh Lofting</a>, but used no material from any of the novels; the main connection is the name and a doctor who can speak to animals, although the <a href="/wiki/List_of_Doctor_Dolittle_characters#The_pushmi-pullyu" title="List of Doctor Dolittle characters">pushmi-pullyu</a>, a much-loved feature of the books, notably makes a very brief appearance in a couple of scenes.
</p><p>The first novel had originally been filmed in 1967 as a <a href="/wiki/Musical_film" title="Musical film">musical</a> under the <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Dolittle_(1967_film)" title="Doctor Dolittle (1967 film)">same title</a>, a closer (albeit still very loose) adaptation of the book. The earlier film was a <a href="/wiki/Box-office_bomb" title="Box-office bomb">box office bomb</a>, but became a <a href="/wiki/Cult_film" title="Cult film">cult classic</a>.
</p><p>The 1998 film was a box office success,<sup id="cite_ref-mojo_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and was received warmly by audiences who praised its humor and thematic profundity. It has become a cult classic in recent years due to Murphy's performance, despite receiving mixed reviews from film critics upon release.<sup id="cite_ref-RT_3-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RT-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-MC_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MC-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> The film's success generated one theatrical <a href="/wiki/Sequel" title="Sequel">sequel</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dr._Dolittle_2" title="Dr. Dolittle 2">Dr. Dolittle 2</a></i>, and three spin-offs: <i><a href="/wiki/Dr._Dolittle_3" title="Dr. Dolittle 3">Dr. Dolittle 3</a></i>, <i><a href="/wiki/Dr._Dolittle:_Tail_to_the_Chief" title="Dr. Dolittle: Tail to the Chief">Dr. Dolittle: Tail to the Chief</a></i>, and <i><a href="/wiki/Dr._Dolittle:_Million_Dollar_Mutts" title="Dr. Dolittle: Million Dollar Mutts">Dr. Dolittle: Million Dollar Mutts</a></i>, all being <a href="/wiki/Direct-to-video" title="Direct-to-video">direct-to-video</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Plot">Plot</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dr._Dolittle_(1998_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Plot">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As a boy, <a href="/wiki/Doctor_Dolittle" title="Doctor Dolittle">John Dolittle</a> displays an ability to talk to and understand <a href="/wiki/Animal" title="Animal">animals</a>, starting with his pet dog. His dog tells him that dogs sniffing each other's butts is how they greet each other, and he does it when meeting his new principal. His behavior greatly disturbs his father Archer, who hires a local minister to perform an <a href="/wiki/Exorcism" title="Exorcism">exorcism</a> on his son, but after John's dog attacks him, Archer sends her away to the dog pound. Upset by this ordeal, John eventually forgets he can talk to animals.
</p><p>As an adult, John becomes a doctor and <a href="/wiki/Surgeon" title="Surgeon">surgeon</a> and is living in <a href="/wiki/San_Francisco" title="San Francisco">San Francisco</a>. He is happily married to his wife Lisa, and has two daughters, typical teenager Charisse, and nerdy Maya, who has a pet <a href="/wiki/Guinea_pig" title="Guinea pig">guinea pig</a> named Rodney, and what she thinks is a <a href="/wiki/Swan" title="Swan">swan</a> egg, which she hopes will bond with her upon hatching. A large medical company owned by Mr. Calloway seeks to buy John's practice, a deal in which one of his colleagues, Dr. Mark Weller, is enthusiastic about. Their other colleague, Dr. Gene Reiss, is skeptical about the deal due to the potential of downsizing patients and staff.
</p><p>John's family goes on vacation, but he must return to work to see a patient, and then pick up Rodney. He accidentally nearly hits a dog with his <a href="/wiki/Nissan_Pathfinder" title="Nissan Pathfinder">car</a>; John bumps his head while the dog gets up and angrily shouts at him in English. The next day, Rodney starts talking to John, who has no memory of his gift, and thinks he is having a mental breakdown. John has a <a href="/wiki/CT_scan" title="CT scan">CT scan</a> after animals start asking for favors when he helps a wounded <a href="/wiki/Owl" title="Owl">owl</a>, and he then unwittingly adopts the dog he ran over, eventually naming him Lucky. John starts secretly helping various animals, including a suicidal circus <a href="/wiki/Bengal_tiger" title="Bengal tiger">tiger</a> named Jake, who feels great cerebral pain. Through all this, John begins learning to re-appreciate his gift, at one point confiding to both Lucky and Mark that he has never felt excited about his work in years. However, Lisa and Mark catch him performing CPR on a <a href="/wiki/Rat" title="Rat">rat</a>, and have him sectioned in a mental hospital.
</p><p>Believing his gift is a hindrance, John rejects all abnormality in his life and returns to work, but in doing so, ostracizes Maya as well, who comes to believe he doesn't like her. Maya admits to Archer that she liked the idea of her father talking to animals. John eavesdrops on the conversation and has a change of heart. John admits to Maya that he does like her for who she is, and encourages her to continue being what she wants to be. John then apologizes to Lucky, and together, they steal Jake from the circus to perform surgery on him. Mark and Gene catch John, but Gene tires of the former's opportunistic attitude and helps John. When Jake is exposed to the party for the buyout, John calmly goes on with the operation. Archer reveals to Lisa that John's gift is real, encouraging her to venture into the operating theatre and keep Jake calm whilst her husband and Gene remove the cause of Jake's pain, saving Jake's life. Calloway is impressed with John's talent, but he declines the deal. 
</p><p>John becomes both a doctor and a veterinarian, embracing his ability to talk to animals. In the film's final scenes, Maya's egg hatches and Rodney thinks it's from <a href="/wiki/Jurassic_Park_(film)" title="Jurassic Park (film)">Jurassic Park</a>, but is revealed to be a baby <a href="/wiki/Alligator" title="Alligator">alligator</a>, and John and Lucky are shown walking to the circus and talking with each other.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Release">Release</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dr._Dolittle_(1998_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Release">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Box_office">Box office</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dr._Dolittle_(1998_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Box office">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On its opening weekend, <i>Dr. Dolittle</i> earned $29,014,324 across 2,777 theaters in the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> and <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, ranking #1 at the box office, the best debut for a Fox film that week. By the end of its run, the film had grossed $144,156,605 in the United States and $150,300,000 internationally, totaling $294,456,605 worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-mojo_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-mojo-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Critical_reception">Critical reception</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Dr._Dolittle_(1998_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Critical reception">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<table class="box-Expand_section plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><a href="/wiki/File:Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg" class="image"><img alt="[icon]" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/20px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png" decoding="async" width="20" height="14" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/30px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1c/Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg/40px-Wiki_letter_w_cropped.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="44" data-file-height="31" /></a></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This section <b>needs expansion</b>. <small>You can help by <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Dr._Dolittle_(1998_film)&amp;action=edit&amp;section=">adding to it</a>.</small>  <small class="date-container"><i>(<span class="date">March 2016</span>)</i></small></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>The film received mixed reviews from critics. The <a href="/wiki/Review_aggregator" title="Review aggregator">review aggregator</a> website <a href="/wiki/Rotten_Tomatoes" title="Rotten Tomatoes">Rotten Tomatoes</a> reported that 42% of critics have given the film a positive review based on 52 reviews, with an <a href="/wiki/Average_rating" class="mw-redirect" title="Average rating">average rating</a> of 5.2/10. The site's critics consensus reads, "<i>Doctor Dolittle</i> finds some mirth in the novelty of wisecracking critters, but this family feature's treacly tone is made queasy by a reliance on scatological gags that undercut the intended warmth."<sup id="cite_ref-RT_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-RT-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> <a href="/wiki/Metacritic" title="Metacritic">Metacritic</a> reports a weighted average score of 46 out of 100 based on 20 critics, indicating "mixed or average reviews".<sup id="cite_ref-MC_4-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MC-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
